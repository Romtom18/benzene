Butadiene Value Chain

Interactive pathways to key industrial polymers and intermediates.

Feedstock: Naphtha / Light Hydrocarbons

↓

Process: Steam Cracking

High-temperature breakdown of hydrocarbons.

↓

Product: Butadiene ($$CH_2=CHCH=CH_2$$)

Extracted from the C4 stream.

Pathway 1: SBR Production

Styrene-Butadiene Rubber

↓

Step 1.1: Copolymerization

$$\underset{\text{Butadiene}}{n(C_4H_6)} + \underset{\text{Styrene}}{m(C_8H_8)} \xrightarrow{\text{Initiator}} \underset{\text{SBR Polymer}}{-[-(C_4H_6)_n-(C_8H_8)_m-]-}$$

↓

Final Use: SBR

  • Passenger Car Tires: The single largest application.
  • Footwear: Soles for shoes and boots.
  • Industrial Goods: Hoses, belts, gaskets.

Pathway 2: PBR Production

Polybutadiene Rubber

↓

Step 2.1: Polymerization

$$\underset{\text{Butadiene}}{n(C_4H_6)} \xrightarrow{\text{Catalyst}} \underset{\text{Polybutadiene}}{-[-CH_2CH=CHCH_2-]_n-}$$

↓

Final Use: PBR

  • Tire Treads & Sidewalls: Improves wear resistance.
  • Impact Modifier: Toughens plastics like Polystyrene (HIPS).
  • Golf Ball Cores: Provides high resilience.

Pathway 3: HMDA Production

For Nylon 6,6

↓

Step 3.1: Hydrocyanation

$$CH_2=CHCH=CH_2 + 2HCN \xrightarrow{\text{Ni Cat.}} \underset{\text{Adiponitrile}}{NC(CH_2)_4CN}$$

↓

Step 3.2: Hydrogenation

$$NC(CH_2)_4CN + 4H_2 \xrightarrow{\text{Raney Ni}} \underset{\text{HMDA}}{H_2N(CH_2)_6NH_2}$$

↓

Final Use: Nylon 6,6

Reacts with Adipic Acid

  • High-performance textiles, automotive parts, zip ties, and carpets.
